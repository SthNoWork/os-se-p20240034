--- Zombie and Orphan Cleanup Notes ---
Q: How are orphans cleaned up? A: Orphans are adopted by init/systemd (PID 1), which eventually calls wait() to clean them up when they finish.
Q: How are zombies cleaned up? A: Zombies are cleaned up when the parent calls wait() to read the child's exit status, removing the entry from the process table.
Q: Can you kill a zombie with 'kill -9'? Why or why not? A: No, because a zombie is already dead. It has no running code to receive signals. It can only be removed when the parent calls wait().
